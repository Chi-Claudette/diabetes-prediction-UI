What is Diabetes?

Diabetes is a disease that occurs when your blood glucose, also called blood sugar, is too high. The hormone insulin made by the pancreas helps glucose get into the cells to be used for energy. In the case of diabetes, the body doesnot make enough—or any—insulin, or doesnot use insulin properly, glucose then stays in the blood and doesnot reach the cells This raises the risk for damage to the eyes, kidneys, nerves, and heart.
Diabetes is a chronic diseases, and is present worldwide. A major reason of deaths in adults across the globe includes this chronic condition. Chronic conditions are also cost associated. A major portion of budget is spent on chronic diseases by governments and individuals

Types of Diabetes

Type 1 Diabetes

It is characterized by deficient insulin production, your body makes little or no insulin. It is usually diagnosed in children and young adults. People with type 1 diabetes requires daily administration of insulin to stay alive.

Type 2 Diabetes

In this case, your body doesn’t use insulin properly and can’t keep blood sugar at normal levels. You can help delay or prevent type 2 diabetes by knowing the risk factors and taking steps toward a healthier lifestyle.

Gestational Diabetes

It develops during pregnancy and usually disappears after giving birth. Women with gestational diabetes are at an increased risk of complications during pregnancy and at delivery. it increases your risk for type 2 diabetes later in life.



Risk factors of Diabetes

Knowing your risk for diabetes will keep you one step ahead of developing complications. You’ll be able to make necessary lifestyle changes to protect yourself from the most serious health effects of the disease.

  • A Body Mass Index value greater than 25
  • Having an inactive or sedentary lifestyle
  • Are over age 45
  • Have an immediate family member with type 2 diabetes
  • Have a lot of belly fat
Early detection of diabets risk factors is very crucial to prevent diabetes related complications such as heart disease, kidney failure, eye damage.
